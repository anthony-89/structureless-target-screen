### Scene 1 · 0:00–0:20 · *The stakes*
> "In heart failure, a protective enzyme — **OPLAH** — gets switched off, a toxic byproduct builds
> up, and the heart takes damage. So switching OPLAH back **on** could be a real therapy — and one
> molecule, **AMP**, does exactly that. But nobody knows **where** AMP binds. Let's find that site —
> **without cheating**."

*(~46 words. Open on the patient, not the protein. Land "without cheating.")*

---

### Scene 2 · 0:20–0:42 · *The bias (why this is different)*
> "Normally you'd find the site by copying it from a related protein. But that **pre-decides the
> answer** — it can only confirm the guess, never discover AMP binds elsewhere. That's the bias we
> removed. Instead: detect **every** pocket across the whole protein, then let AMP dock **freely**
> and choose its own site."

*(~51 words. The thesis — slow down. Land "pre-decides the answer.")*

---

### Scene 3 · 0:42–1:07 · *One pocket, three methods*
> "Searching the entire structure by geometry, two independent tools — **P2Rank** and **fpocket** —
> agree on one dominant pocket. Then the real test: we dock AMP with **no hints at all**, free to
> land anywhere. It drops straight into that same pocket, **two ångström** away. Three independent
> methods, **one answer** — a discovery, not an assumption."

*(~57 words. Build to "one answer." Let the convergence graphic finish.)*

---

### Scene 4 · 1:07–1:34 · *AMP's binding site* (camera flies into the pocket)
> "Watch AMP settle into the pocket it chose. **Seventeen** residues wrap around it — a glycine-rich
> phosphate cleft carrying the motif **D-x-G-G-T**, twice, one for each of OPLAH's two nucleotide
> domains. And the pocket draws residues from position **thirteen all the way to twelve-eighty-eight**
> — so a tool that truncates the protein literally breaks this site."

*(~60 words. As you say "watch AMP settle," the camera zooms in: AMP is the cyan molecule, the 17 contact residues are the orange dots.)*

---

### Scene 5 · 1:34–2:01 · *Two-arm screen* (the hit drops into the same pocket)
> "Now — what else fits here? The nucleotide arm recovers natural mimics like **dGMP**, plus two
> approved antivirals, entecavir and sofosbuvir. Then, from **five thousand** diverse compounds —
> here, in green, a brand-new scaffold drops into AMP's own site. **Seven hundred seventy-five**
> out-score AMP; **three hundred seventy-nine** are genuinely novel — all **leads to test in an
> assay**, not measured affinities."

*(~62 words. "in green" = the on-screen hit (ZINC4126706) settling into AMP's pocket. "leads to test in an assay" is the honesty + the first future-work beat.)*

### Scene 6 · 2:01–2:26 · *Two Claudes, one loop* (the 25% "Claude Use" beat)

### Scene 7 · 2:26–2:55 · *What we achieved · why it generalizes · what's next*
> "So — five questions: the **structure**, the **pockets**, the **binding site**, the **mimics**,
> and **better binders** — all answered, and a hidden bias removed. Because the method assumes
> **nothing** about the target, it's **designed to generalize**: give it any structureless target
> with a known modulator, and it aims to return a shortlist — OPLAH is the first test. Next: the wet
> lab — does a hit activate OPLAH? Open-source, reproducible — **built with Claude**."

*(~60 words. Three beats at once — achieved (5 questions), why it generalizes, and what's next (the assay). End on "built with Claude.")*
